Menu

Dosa
Rs.50

Dosa is a thin, savory crepe from South India, made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils. It's a popular breakfast or snack, often served with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and chutneys (like coconut or tomato). Varieties include plain dosa, masala dosa (stuffed with potatoes), and neer dosa (water dosa). Dosa is enjoyed throughout India and increasingly worldwide.

Momos
Rs.60

Momos are steamed or fried dumplings popular in South Asian cuisine, particularly in Nepal, Tibet, and India. They typically consist of a dough wrapper filled with minced meat or vegetables, and are often served with a spicy dipping sauce. Momos are a beloved street food and can be found in various settings, from local eateries to upscale restaurants.

Noodles
Rs.70

Noodles are a type of food made from unleavened dough, typically wheat-based, that is formed into long, thin strips or other shapes. They are a staple in many cultures worldwide, featuring prominently in both Western (pasta) and Eastern (Chinese, Japanese, etc.) cuisines. Noodles are incredibly versatile, prepared by boiling, frying, or baking, and are often served with a variety of sauces, meats, and vegetables.

Kebabs
Rs.80

Kebabs are pieces of meat, fish, or vegetables, often marinated, skewered, and grilled or roasted. Originating in the Middle East, kebabs have become a popular dish worldwide, with various regional adaptations. From shish kebabs on skewers to doner kebabs in flatbread, the term "kebab" encompasses a wide range of grilled or roasted meat dishes.

Burger
Rs.90

A burger is a sandwich consisting of a cooked patty, typically beef, placed between two halves of a bun, often accompanied by toppings like lettuce, tomato, and cheese. It's a popular and versatile meal, enjoyed globally at fast food restaurants, casual gatherings, or even at home. Burgers can be customized with a wide array of ingredients and sauces, making them a beloved and adaptable food choice.

Chicken Biryani
Rs.100

Chicken biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish popular in South Asia, particularly in India. It typically features long-grain basmati rice, marinated chicken, and a blend of fragrant spices, often cooked using the dum method which seals in the flavors. The dish is known for its rich taste and is commonly served with raita or a side of gravy.
